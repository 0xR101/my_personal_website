I am a PhD researcher in Theoretical Physics at King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals (KFUPM), specializing in Condensed Matter Physics and quantum information processing. My doctoral research is currently conducted at the Interdisciplinary Research Center for Advanced Quantum Computing, under the supervision of Professor Daniel Loss.

My academic journey is driven by a deep interest in the fundamental mechanics of quantum systems and the physical realization of quantum computation. Prior to my doctoral studies, I completed my undergraduate degree with highest honors (3.96/4.00 CGPA), developing a rigorous foundation in physics alongside advanced computational and engineering methodologies. During my senior year, I also served as the President of the Physics Club, fostering academic engagement within the student body.

### Research Interests

My primary research investigates the dynamics of solid-state quantum systems, with a specific focus on:
* **Spin Qubits & Semiconductor Quantum Dots:** Analyzing Hamiltonian dynamics, coherence times, and high-fidelity state manipulation.
* **Reversible Computing:** Exploring the thermodynamic limits of computation and energy efficiency in quantum circuits.
* **Quantum Circuit Design:** Implementing theoretical models into computational simulations to benchmark gate operations.

### Academic Experience & Collaborations

I actively collaborate with international institutions to bridge the gap between theoretical models and experimental fabrication. 
* **Institute for Quantum Computing (IQC), University of Waterloo:** Completed a three-month research residency focused on the evaluation and modeling of fabricated qubits.
* **NCCR SPIN:** Upcoming research collaboration (June 2026) focusing on advanced spin-based quantum architectures.
* **King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals:** Teaching Assistant for advanced physics and quantum computing coursework (PHYS590), overseeing student instruction and academic administration.
