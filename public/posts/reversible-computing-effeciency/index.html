Energy Efficiency in Reversible Quantum Circuits

March 22, 2026 1 min read
Research Reversible Computing Thermodynamics

Landauer’s principle establishes a fundamental lower bound on the energy consumption of irreversible computation. By employing reversible computing architectures, we theoretically circumvent this thermodynamic limit.

This research focuses on the optimization of quantum logic gates to minimize energy dissipation.

Current Benchmarks

Recent simulations indicate a significant reduction in gate error rates when applying optimized pulse sequences. The transition from classical logical irreversibility to quantum reversibility requires a paradigm shift in how we approach circuit design.

“Information is physical.” — Rolf Landauer

More detailed findings from our recent simulations will be presented at the upcoming symposium.
